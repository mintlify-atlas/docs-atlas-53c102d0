description: "All CPU and GPU hardware flavors for Hugging Face compute jobs, with vCPU, RAM, and VRAM specs to help you pick the right instance for your workload."
---

When the agent launches a training or inference job via `hf_jobs`, it targets a specific hardware flavor. Each flavor determines the number of virtual CPUs, the amount of system RAM, and ‚Äî for GPU instances ‚Äî the amount of GPU VRAM available to your job. Choosing the right flavor avoids out-of-memory failures and keeps costs proportional to your workload.

The default timeout for all jobs is **30 minutes**. Training jobs almost always need a longer timeout ‚Äî set it explicitly with the `timeout` parameter (e.g. `"4h"`, `"8h"`, `"24h"`).

## CPU hardware

Use CPU instances for data preprocessing, lightweight inference, or any task that does not require a GPU.

| Flavor | vCPU | RAM |
|---|---|---|
| `cpu-basic` | 2 | 16 GB |
| `cpu-upgrade` | 8 | 32 GB |

## GPU hardware

Use GPU instances for model training, fine-tuning, and GPU-accelerated inference. The VRAM column is the amount of GPU memory available to your job ‚Äî this is the primary constraint for loading model weights and fitting batches.

| Flavor | vCPU | RAM | GPU VRAM |
|---|---|---|---|
| `t4-small` | 4 | 15 GB | 16 GB |
| `t4-medium` | 8 | 30 GB | 16 GB |
| `a10g-small` | 4 | 15 GB | 24 GB |
| `a10g-large` | 12 | 46 GB | 24 GB |
| `a10g-largex2` | 24 | 92 GB | 48 GB |
| `a10g-largex4` | 48 | 184 GB | 96 GB |
| `a100-large` | 12 | 142 GB | 80 GB |
| `a100x4` | 48 | 568 GB | 320 GB |
| `a100x8` | 96 | 1,136 GB | 640 GB |
| `l4x1` | 8 | 30 GB | 24 GB |
| `l4x4` | 48 | 186 GB | 96 GB |
| `l40sx1` | 8 | 62 GB | 48 GB |
| `l40sx4` | 48 | 382 GB | 192 GB |
| `l40sx8` | 192 | 1,534 GB | 384 GB |

<Note>
  `a10g-small` and `a10g-large` have the same 24 GB GPU. The difference is CPU and RAM only. Choose `a10g-large` if your data loading or preprocessing is CPU-bound.
</Note>

| Flavor | Description |

| `inf2x6` | AWS Inferentia2 accelerator instance for cost-efficient inference at scale. |

## Choosing hardware for your workload

The right flavor depends on your model size and the task you are running.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Small models (1‚Äì3B params)" icon="microchip">
    Start with `t4-small` (16 GB VRAM). If you need more CPU or system RAM for data loading, move to `t4-medium`.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Medium models (7‚Äì13B params)" icon="server">
    Use `a10g-large` (24 GB VRAM) for 7B models with LoRA. For 13B full fine-tuning, consider `a100-large` (80 GB VRAM).
  </Card>
  <Card title="Large models (30B+)" icon="database">
    Use `a100-large` (80 GB VRAM) for 30B models. For 70B+, use multi-GPU flavors like `a100x4` or `l40sx4`.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Inference at scale" icon="bolt">
    Use `inf2x6` for cost-efficient inference workloads. For standard GPU inference, `l4x1` or `l40sx1` offer good throughput per dollar.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Timeout guidance

The default timeout of `30m` is appropriate only for quick tasks like data inspection or short inference runs. For training jobs, set a timeout that exceeds your estimated runtime:

| Workload | Recommended timeout |
|---|---|
| Data preprocessing, inspection | `30m`‚Äì`1h` |
| 1‚Äì3B model fine-tuning | `3h`‚Äì`4h` |
| 7‚Äì13B model fine-tuning | `6h`‚Äì`8h` |
| 30B+ model fine-tuning | `12h`‚Äì`24h` |

<Warning>
  If a training job hits its timeout mid-run, it is cancelled and all local job storage is lost. Always set `push_to_hub=True` in your training config and provide a `hub_model_id` so checkpoints are saved to your Hugging Face repository before the job ends.
</Warning>
